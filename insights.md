# Patterns & Insights — Cold Outreach Research
### Synthesized from 10 experts, 15 videos, 5 LinkedIn creators, 4 blogs
*April 2026*

---

## The Core Shift Happening Right Now

Cold outreach is going through the same maturation that paid media went
through a decade ago. The teams that figured out paid media early didn't
win by spending more — they won by testing faster and targeting tighter.

The same logic is now playing out in outbound. The winning teams in 2026
are not the ones with the biggest lists. They are the ones running the
most focused micro-campaigns, using AI to personalize at a depth that
was previously only possible manually, and iterating on what works faster
than anyone else.

This is the central pattern that runs across every source in this research.

---

## Insight 1 — Volume Is No Longer the Lever

Every expert in this research, without exception, has moved away from
high-volume generic blasting. Eric Nowoslawski sends 4 million emails
a month but runs 250 Clay tables simultaneously — each one a tightly
segmented micro-campaign with a specific angle.

The implication for 100Hires: a list of 500 well-segmented leads with
a signal-based angle will outperform a list of 5,000 generic contacts
every time. The bottleneck is not reach. It is relevance.

**Hypothesis to test:** Campaigns segmented by a specific hiring signal
(e.g. companies that posted a role 30+ days ago with no hire) will
outperform generic "HR decision maker" campaigns by at least 3x on
reply rate.

---

## Insight 2 — Infrastructure Is the Invisible Bottleneck

Hans Dekker's content makes a point that almost no one talks about
publicly: most cold email campaigns fail before a human ever reads them.
SEGs (Secure Email Gateways) like Proofpoint and Mimecast filter
outbound at the corporate level. Behavioral signals like "likely to mark
as spam" can be detected at the lead level before sending.

Most teams skip this layer entirely and then wonder why their carefully
crafted emails aren't converting.

**Hypothesis to test:** Running a pre-send deliverability filter that
removes SEG-protected leads and "hostile prospect" flags will increase
effective open rate by 20-30% without changing a single word of copy.

## Insight 3 — Personalization Has Two Levels

There is a false consensus in the market that personalization means
inserting a few data points into a template. Felix Frank calls this
out directly — "that's just noise."

The real split is between:
- **Surface personalization** — first name, company, job title tokens
- **Deep personalization** — understanding what the person is actually
  responsible for, what they're dealing with right now, and writing
  to that specifically

Zahra Batool Butt's 166% reply rate improvement came from one thing:
making the prospect feel genuinely understood rather than targeted.

**Hypothesis to test:** Emails that open with a role-specific pain
observation (no name, no company mention) will outperform emails that
open with a company-specific data point by a meaningful margin — because
the former feels like insight, the latter feels like research.

---

## Insight 4 — The Signal Layer Is Underused

Multiple experts reference intent signals and hiring signals as the
highest-leverage targeting layer available — yet most teams are not
using them at all.

Hiring signals in particular are directly relevant to 100Hires:
- A company posting 5+ roles simultaneously is in active hiring pain
- A role that has been open 30+ days signals a broken process
- A company switching ATS vendors shows intent to solve the problem

These signals are available for free through LinkedIn Jobs and Indeed.
No paid tools required to get started.

**Hypothesis to test:** A campaign targeting companies with 3+ open
roles that have been posted for 30+ days will have 2x the reply rate
of a campaign targeting the same ICP without that signal filter.

---

## Insight 5 — The CTA Is Where Most Campaigns Die

Across all 10 experts, the consistent failure point they identify is
the CTA — specifically asking for too much too soon.

"Book a 30-minute demo" as a first touch CTA is the equivalent of
proposing marriage on a first date. Felix Frank, Zahra Batool Butt and
Fernando Cao Zheng all independently land on the same solution: make
the first ask so small it requires almost no commitment.

The "Reply HIRE" CTA from our SOP is a good example of this principle
in action — it asks for a single word, not a calendar booking.

**Hypothesis to test:** A soft asset CTA ("reply for the case study")
will generate more total pipeline than a direct meeting CTA ("book
a 15-min call") even if the direct CTA books more calls — because
the asset CTA creates more total conversations that can be converted.

## What This Means for 100Hires Specifically

100Hires is targeting companies that use HiringThing and PrismHR for instance.
That is already a strong signal filter — technographic targeting.

The next layer of signal to add:
1. **Hiring volume** — how many roles are they currently posting?
2. **Role age** — how long have those roles been open?
3. **G2 sentiment** — are their employees complaining about their
   current ATS in reviews?

A company using HiringThing + posting 5 roles + one role open 45 days
+ a 3-star G2 review mentioning "too manual" is not a cold lead.
That is a warm lead who does not know 100Hires exists yet.

That is the targeting layer that turns a cold outreach campaign into
a pipeline engine.

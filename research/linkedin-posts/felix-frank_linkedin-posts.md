## Post 1 — 5-Step Process for Message-Market Fit

**Theme:** Finding message-market fit for cold email across 50+ clients

Winning with cold email comes down to one thing: finding the right
message. Here's the 5-step process tested across 50+ clients at
StackOptimise:

**Step 1 — Segment your audience**
- Different personas = different messaging
- Segment based on job title, industry, company size
- Tailor your approach based on who you're reaching

**Step 2 — Test strategies in parallel**
- Run 10-15 different angles simultaneously
- Focus on testing the offer
- Also test triggers, tone, CTAs, copywriting styles

**Step 3 — Combine winning messages**
- Take best-performing templates across all campaigns
- Stack them into a 3-4 step sequence
- Each email pulls from something already validated

**Step 4 — Scale and squeeze until performance drops**
- Run the winning sequence hard
- Extract every bit of performance you can

**Step 5 — Repeat from Step 2**
- Results will inevitably dip over time
- Once reply rate falls below 50% of original, restart from Step 2

**Key insight:** This cycle is how you build predictable, scalable
outbound. Never stop testing — even winning sequences have a shelf life.

## Post 2 — TAM Coverage Strategy

**Theme:** Most companies are barely scratching the surface of their market

Most companies have zero strategy for actually covering their TAM.
They're running the same playbook: hire a few sales reps, give them
HubSpot sequences, let them figure it out.

**A typical conversation:**
- "How many contacts are your team reaching each month?" — "Around 2,000"
- "How big is your TAM?" — "Roughly 200,000"
- "So it's going to take you over 8 years to reach your market once?"
- "...yeah, I guess so"

**The fix — reverse engineer from your TAM:**
To maximize cold email, hit your entire TAM every 3 months. This
aligns with how buying actually works. At any given time, only a
small percentage of your market is actively looking. If you're not
consistently cycling through your TAM, you're missing those buyers
entirely.

This applies regardless of TAM size:
- 1,000 prospects
- 50,000 prospects
- 1,000,000+ prospects

The principle doesn't change. Only the scale of execution does.

**Key insight:** Most companies don't actually have an outbound
problem. They're just not reaching enough of their market, often
enough.

## Post 3 — Real Personalisation vs Noise

**Theme:** What genuine cold email personalisation actually looks like

You know your emails are good when prospects reply just to ask how
you wrote them.

**The frame for crafting copy:**
How can we put this message in front of the ideal prospect in a way
they've never seen before from anyone who's sent them a cold email?
Unique. Personalised. Pattern-disrupting. Humanised.

**What fake personalisation looks like:**
"Hey John, saw you went to Leeds Uni, anyway here's my pitch..."
That's not personalisation. That's just noise.

**What real personalisation looks like:**
Writing a long letter to a family member. Detailed. Thoughtful.
Genuinely considered.

**Key insight:** When you do this properly, the prospect doesn't just
read your email — they feel something. They think: "This person has
actually taken time to understand me." When you can evoke this
emotion, you don't need to rely on the strength of the offer as much.
The trust is already there. If you can't build the perfect offer yet,
use thoughtful personalisation to make them feel like you truly get
them. That's the key.

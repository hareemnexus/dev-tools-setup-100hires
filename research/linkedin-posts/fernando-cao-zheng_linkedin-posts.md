## Post 1 — GTM Flywheel Framework

**Theme:** AI-native GTM pipeline architecture

Stop running your GTM like a funnel. Every closed deal should make
the next one easier. Here's a 5-stage flywheel that does exactly that.

Most companies run GTM like this: Awareness → Decision → Conversion →
Start over. Every closed deal means starting from scratch. No
compounding. No leverage. No momentum. A flywheel fixes that.

**5 Stages:**

1. **Signal** — Track buyer intent before they raise their hand.
   Tools: Bombora, 6sense, Cognism

2. **Enrich** — Waterfall 150+ data sources into verified contacts.
   Tools: Clay, ZoomInfo, Findymail. Cost per verified lead: under $0.02

3. **Outbound** — Personalized multi-channel at scale.
   Tools: Smartlead, Instantly.ai (email), lemlist, HeyReach.io,
   Expandi (LinkedIn)

4. **Content** — Build authority that compounds. Every post generates
   impressions. Every impression warms the next outbound touch.

5. **Convert** — Book meetings and close.
   Tools: HubSpot or Salesforce (CRM), Gong (call intelligence),
   Slack (deal notifications)

**Key insight:** Anthropic's Claude Code sits in the center. It
orchestrates the enrichment pipeline, writes campaign copy, generates
content, and connects every stage through n8n, Zapier, or Make.

Each motion feeds the next: Signal data improves outbound targeting →
Outbound conversations inform content topics → Content builds trust
that increases reply rates → Closed deals create referrals that
generate new signals.

## Post 2 — 8 Cold Email Frameworks

**Theme:** Cold email copywriting frameworks and formulas

Most people write cold emails like: "Hi, I'd love to show you our
platform." That's not strategy. That's a template with a first name.

**8 proven cold email formulas:**

1. **PAS** — Problem, Agitate, Solve. Lead with their pain, not your product.
2. **BAB** — Before, After, Bridge. Paint the transformation they want.
3. **AIDA** — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action. Classic funnel, email-sized.
4. **3Cs** — Compliment, Case Study, CTA. Alex Berman's relevance + proof formula.
5. **4Ts** — Trigger, Think, Third-Party, Talk. Josh Braun's signal-first approach.
6. **REPLY** — Results, Empathy, Personalize, Laser, You. Jason Bay's
   Outbound Squad email checklist.
7. **SPPC** — Signal, Problem, Proof, Soft CTA. Eric Nowoslawski's
   Cold IQ volume formula.
8. **QVC** — Question, Value Prop, CTA. Three sentences. Done.

**Key insight:** The difference between 100 emails per reply and 10
emails per reply isn't the tool. It's the framework. Pick one. Test it
for 500 sends. Measure reply rate. Iterate.

## Post 3 — The One-Person Billion Dollar Company

**Theme:** AI replacing traditional GTM headcount and architecture

Sam Altman predicted the first one-person billion-dollar company.
Matthew Gallagher, 41, built Medvi from his house with $20K and two
months. A telehealth platform. $401M revenue by end of year one.
Projecting $1.8B this year. With 2 people.

**Old GTM playbook:**
- Hire 5-10 SDRs at $65K+ each
- Buy seats on Apollo, ZoomInfo, Clay, Outreach, SalesLoft
- 3 months to ramp before anyone books a meeting
- SDRs manually research leads, send 50 emails a day

**New GTM playbook:**
- AI classifies every lead against ICP before you spend on enrichment
- Automated systems verify emails across multiple sources
- Campaigns go live in hours, not weeks
- Domain health monitoring keeps you out of spam
- One system replaces the research, qualification, and outreach of
  an entire SDR floor

**Key insight:** 5 SDRs at $65K each with 4-5 tool seats and 3 months
ramp = $500K+ before results. The new architecture does the same work
in hours. The barrier to scale isn't headcount anymore. It's
architecture.

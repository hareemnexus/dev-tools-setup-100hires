## Post 1 — 36 Replies in 12 Hours: What Worked

**Theme:** Cold email angles that drive replies — humour, soft triggers, tone

Launched a campaign and got 36 replies in under 12 hours. Variant 3
got 166% more replies than the other 2 combined. Here's why:

**1. The humour was vivid**
For the opener, use something that makes the reader smile.
- "brace yourself for an unsexy topic"
- "try not to nod off"

**2. The trigger was soft and universal**
Not "I saw your funding round" — everyone says that. Something quieter.
- "I imagine you're quite busy with [recent thing they're dealing with]"

**3. The tone was understated**
Never put pressure on the reader. Be polite.
- "do you mind if I share something useful?"

**Key insight:** B2B buyers reply to emails that feel like a person
accidentally found them on the web. Pattern disruption through
humour and soft triggers outperforms hard personalization hooks
that everyone else is already using.

## Post 2 — Event Invite Campaign: 1 Positive Every 48 Leads

**Theme:** Underrated cold email campaign type — event invites done right

Event invite emails are among the most ignored in outbound. Ran one
anyway. January to February:
- 11,158 leads contacted
- 228 positive replies
- 1 positive every 48 leads

**Why it worked — they did the opposite of everyone else:**

Most invite emails are structured:
- 1st line: here's the event
- 2nd line: here's why it's great
- 3rd line: please come

Their structure:
- 1st line: the invite
- 2nd line onward: everything about them — their recent wins, what
  they're actually doing at the company, what they're responsible for
- Final line: connecting their background to why they specifically
  would be a great fit

**Key insight:** "Based on what you've built, you'd actually add
something here." That's what disarms people. They're so used to being
pitched at that a genuine invite catches them off guard. Event
campaigns are massively underrated — most people don't even try them.

## Post 3 — 10 Questions Before Hitting Send

**Theme:** Pre-send quality checklist used across 2 million cold emails

At StackOptimise, sent 2 million cold emails last month. Got replies
from Nestle, The Economist, and more global names. 1 positive reply
every 175 emails sent. A big part of that comes down to 10 questions
asked before hitting send on anything:

1. Would I actually reply to this?
2. Does the subject line sound like a sales email?
3. Does the prospect know what's in it for them?
4. Can a stranger understand this with zero context?
5. Do I have a clear reason to contact THIS person specifically?
6. Am I asking for too much too soon?
7. Can it be read on a phone in one scroll?
8. Would I cringe if a good copywriter saw this?
9. Is line one proof of real research, or does it just sound like it?
10. Is the CTA low friction, or basically a marriage proposal?

**Key insight:** These 10 questions act as a quality gate that filters
out lazy outreach before it damages sender reputation. The difference
between 1 reply per 48 sends and 1 per 175 sends often comes down to
how honestly you answer question 1.
